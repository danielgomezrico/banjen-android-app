## Executive Summary

- **Five personas, five goals, one shared sacred rule.** Across every research file, the universal dealbreaker is **ads on the tuning surface** — confirmed in v2 user interviews, in Reclame Aqui complaints, in Tuner ONE "avalanche de anúncios" reviews, in Cifra Club one-stars, in Perfect Tuner "intrusive popups" reviews, and corroborated by academic findings that older adults are empirically more disrupted by interruptive ads than younger cohorts (Wildenbos et al., 2018; Zha & Wu, 2014). All five personas walk away on the same condition. The tuning screen is not "a feature to polish" — it is the only screen that *must not break*.
- **The DGBD ecosystem is larger than any single instrument.** Banjen's reference tones (D3, G3, B3, D4) serve at least four user populations: 4-string DGBD/Chicago banjo, the lower 4 of a 5-string banjo, baritone-uke retunes, and — critically — Brazilian/Portuguese **cavaquinho**, where DGBD is the published standard ([market-analyst.md], [community-listener.md] quote #33, [academic-researcher.md] §5). The cavaquinho pool alone (BR + diaspora) is 5–10× larger than the entire global 4-string banjo population.
- **Primary persona is unchanged but its motivation has been re-validated by literature.** Harold M. remains the beachhead. Academic research now provides external corroboration: Balbag et al. (2014, 64% dementia-risk reduction), Bisiacchi et al. (2025, ASPREE cohort, 35%), and Bugos et al. (2022) make Harold's "fighting dementia" framing defensible, not folkloric. His value proposition is **cognitive maintenance**, not hobbyism.
- **The new persona of v3 is Lúcia G.** — a São Paulo-based cavaquinho student who has tried 3 ad-laden Brazilian tuner apps and rage-quit each. She is both an opportunity (a million-strong adjacent market) and an empathy check (her pain is identical to Harold's, in a different language).
- **The skeptic persona has been promoted, not retired.** Marcus T. (the studio musician who installed Banjen, hit the ad layout shift, and deleted it) is preserved but reframed as the *Acquired-and-Lost* archetype. He represents every download that doesn't convert. New supporting evidence from competitor-analyst.md (LikeTones marketing claim "No Ads, No Purchases, No Registration") shows Marcus has competitive alternatives that already meet his bar — Banjen's window to win him back is narrow.

## Persona 1: Harold M., 67 — *The Retired Beginner (Primary)*

*Retired accountant returning to music after 50 years; banjo practice is a daily cognitive-health routine he won't tell his wife is medicinal.*

**Location:** Suburban Knoxville, Tennessee
**Income:** $55,000/year (pension + Social Security)
**Tech savviness:** **2/5** — Comfortable with calls/texts/YouTube/weather. Grandkids configure new apps. Anxious about anything unfamiliar.
**Decision authority:** Full, but compares every app purchase to a physical product ("Is this worth more than a set of strings?").

**Research evidence trail:**
- [market-analyst.md] §Segment Sizing — 4-string DGBD "Chicago" tuning is ~5–15k players globally; the broader 5-string DGBD overlap pool is ~200–350k, all addressable by Banjen's existing tones.
- [community-listener.md] Theme 4 (#28, #30, #31) — older-beginner anxiety, USF cognitive-music study, retiree banjo uptake all corroborate the persona.
- [academic-researcher.md] §2 — Balbag et al. (2014), Bisiacchi et al. (2025), Bugos et al. (2022) — dementia-risk associations of 22–64%.
- [social-analyst.md] — English-speaking banjo tuner searchers skew 50+, male-heavy, US Appalachia / NC concentration. Banjo Hangout demographic survey.
- [competitor-analyst.md] §Top-3 Teardown — NETIGEN Master Banjo Tuner: 100K+ installs but 1-star ad-rage reviews; "every NETIGEN one-star is a Harold M. who would convert to Banjen if discovery worked."

### Goals
- **Primary:** Play well enough to join the Thursday evening bluegrass jam circle at the community center.
- **Secondary:** Maintain a structured daily routine that replaces the discipline of his 35-year accounting career.
- **Hidden:** Cognitive insurance. After watching Linda's mother decline from dementia, he read AARP and is quietly practising as brain exercise. He hasn't told Linda. This is the stake the product is actually serving — confirmed by academic-researcher.md's evidence base.

### Pain Points
- **Biggest frustration:** Cannot tell if his banjo is in tune — but suspects he could if he had a reliable looping reference (dormant ear from 50-years-ago trumpet band).
- **Daily friction:** YouTube reference-tone workaround = 30-sec unskippable ads, talking heads, kitchen noise, rewinds that trigger more ads. 15 minutes of friction for 45 seconds of usable tone.
- **What keeps him up at night:** Not money — *discipline*. "If I get frustrated enough to skip a day, then two days, then a week — that scares me more than sounding bad at the jam." Tuning friction is a crack in his brain-health regimen.
- **Previous failed attempts:** GuitarTuna (paywall before banjo trial — confirmed by competitor-analyst.md: banjo is gated behind Pro), Snark clip-on ("needle jumps around"), generic chromatic ("assumes I know the target notes"), YouTube ("ads kill the experience").

### Behaviors
- **Current workaround:** Asks teacher Ray to tune at $40 lessons — $6–7/lesson goes to tuning = $25–30/month tax.
- **Tools:** Samsung Galaxy, YouTube (85% of 50–64 use it, Pew 2025), Banjo Hangout (reads, rarely posts), Facebook jam-circle group, spiral notebook.
- **Online communities:** YouTube + Banjo Hangout. Banjo Hangout's aging, high-trust demographic ([social-analyst.md] influencer landscape) is *exactly* him.
- **Decision triggers:** Ray says "try this." A jam friend hands him a phone. Teacher recommendations are the #1 acquisition channel for this cohort.

### A Day in Their Life
Harold wakes at 6:30, reads about music and brain health (won't tell Linda), and sits down with his Deering Goodtime at 7:00 sharp. He types "D G B D banjo reference tone" into YouTube, waits through a truck ad, then a fellow talks for two minutes before playing a note. Linda starts the dishwasher. He rewinds, gets another ad. Fifteen minutes of phone-fighting for forty-five seconds of reference tone. He plays his exercises knowing they sound "a little off." The frustration dims something deeper he won't name.

### In Their Own Words
> "I spent fifteen minutes fighting with my phone and maybe forty-five seconds actually listening to reference tones."
*Source: v2 user-personas.md, Harold M. interview Q1*

> "When something as basic as tuning creates a barrier, it's not just annoying. It's threatening the whole routine... For me, it's staying sharp. Staying myself."
*Source: v2 user-personas.md, Harold M. interview Q10*

> *"Older students have a tendency to feel they need to play perfectly now, pressuring themselves with the mindset of 'I'm taking it up later in life so I need to catch on quick.'"*
*Source: community-listener.md, quote #28 (Banjo Hangout "Learning banjo, is it harder for older people")*

> *"Research from the University of South Florida found that seniors who participated in a 10-week program learning musical instruments showed greater gains in cognitive function than those who engaged in other leisure activities, including crossword puzzles."*
*Source: community-listener.md, quote #30 (Graying With Grace, citing USF study)*

### Product Fit
- **Why he'd choose it:** Four buttons, four strings, looping tones, no needle, no mic permission, no signup. Matches every older-adult UX rule in academic-researcher.md §4 (single-purpose, ≥48dp targets, error-tolerant, no interruptive ads). $40-lesson tuning tax makes $3–5 a clear win.
- **Why he'd walk away:** A single bad ad experience = permanent deletion. Account creation = "I'm tuning a banjo, not applying for a mortgage." Unfamiliar music terminology. Any whiff of subscription.
- **Willingness to pay:** $3–5 one-time. Subscriptions are a non-starter on fixed income.

**Hidden insight (carried from v2, re-validated):** His dream feature is the *opposite* of retention — he wants Banjen to make him independent of itself. "Tell me 'you're close' or 'way off' — almost like a patient teacher sitting next to me." This reframes strategy from engagement KPIs to empowerment outcomes.

### Pain Points

- **What keeps her up at night:** Being judged in the roda. "Tar desafinada num bate-papo de samba é constrangedor — é como chegar atrasada numa reunião." (Implied from community-listener.md Theme 1 and Theme 5.)
- **Previous failed attempts:** Afinador Cifra Club (subscription rage, ads, "tuner stops working mid-string" — community-listener.md #13), Tuner ONE (ad avalanche — #11), Afinador Cavaquinho (mic-based, fails in bar noise), her cousin's voice-memo workaround (Rafael's friend Thiago's trick — v2 personas).

### Behaviors

- **Tools:** Samsung Galaxy A-series, WhatsApp (the "internet of Brazil"), YouTube, Instagram, Spotify, Cifra Club (browser), Mercado Livre.
- **Online communities:** WhatsApp samba groups (60+ members), Instagram cavaquinho hashtags (#cavaquinho, #cavaco, #samba), YouTube comment sections on Professor Damiro and similar teachers. **Does not use Reddit, does not use Banjo Hangout.**
- **Decision triggers:** A WhatsApp share. A Professor Damiro video that says "esse afinador funciona." A Brazilian YouTuber she trusts in a description box. A Portuguese-language Play Store listing — translated, not just localized — that says "afinador de cavaquinho" in the title.

She opens Afinador Cavaquinho — it loads with a full-screen interstitial, she hunts for the X, finds it, and the app then crashes. She tries again. The mic picks up the football commentary instead of her cavaco. **Hidden insight:** Her advocacy radius is huge. A WhatsApp share to her samba group reaches 60+ people, each of whom has their own group. Brazilian app-virality runs on WhatsApp, not Twitter or app-store reviews. Banjen's CAC on this channel is effectively zero *if* the cultural framing is right.

## Persona 3: Eileen B., 58 — *The Irish Tenor Session Player (Evolved from Siobhan)*

*Galway-based primary teacher and Irish tenor banjo player whose Tuesday session at a local pub is the social anchor of her week; tunes GDAE, not DGBD.*

**Location:** Galway, Ireland (representative of the Irish trad ecosystem; ~30–60k tenor banjo players globally per market-analyst.md)
**Income:** EUR 47,000/year (primary teacher)
**Tech savviness:** **4/5** — comfortable with multiple music apps (Tunefox, Pro Metronome, OAIM, Voice Memos). In session, wants *zero* visible tech.
**Decision authority:** Full + amplification. One post on TheSession.org reaches "Dublin to Boston to Melbourne overnight." (v2 Siobhan insight, corroborated by social-analyst.md influencer landscape.)

**Research evidence trail:**
- [market-analyst.md] §Segment Sizing — 4-string tenor (Irish) GDAE = ~30–60k global; stable, anchored by session culture; no growth signal but no decline either.
- [community-listener.md] Theme 6 (#40, #41, #43) — GDAE is the Irish tenor standard; Mandolin Cafe documents low-G GDAE preference; the Session forum is the cultural hub.
- [competitor-analyst.md] §Direct Competitor Table — only one dedicated GDAE tenor banjo app (PK Development "Tenor Banjo Tuner," 10K+, mic-only, low-polish). Banjen's reference-tone moat applies cleanly here.
- [social-analyst.md] §Search Demand Heatmap — Irish trad ("GDAE") is small absolute volume, very high engagement, clustered around few influencers (Enda Scahill, Banjo Lemonade, Joff Lowson). Ideal influencer-marketing target *once* GDAE preset ships.
- [academic-researcher.md] §3 — session-context "competence" need under SDT: any latency that undermines visible competence in front of peers is fatal to retention.

### Goals
- **Primary:** Stay perfectly in tune during fast-paced pub sessions where there are ~25 seconds between tune sets — and stopping the set to re-tune is socially unacceptable.
- **Secondary:** One reference-tone tool that handles her tenor banjo *and* her octave mandolin (both GDAE) — replacing the tuning forks she keeps physically losing.
- **Hidden:** Identity as the trusted gear-recommender in her session network. She *wants* to recommend — but only something she'd defend publicly on TheSession.org. Influence is her social currency.

### Pain Points
- **Biggest frustration:** The "15-second fumble gap" between realising she's out of tune and hearing a reference tone. Every second of fumbling is a second stolen from the 25-second between-sets window.
- **Daily friction:** Tuning forks are acoustically perfect and silent (the right tool, sonically) but they roll under tables and end up in the school hoover. She's on her 4th A fork this year. The app needs to be *less losable*, not better-sounding, than a fork.
- **What keeps her up at night:** "If you're fumbling with an app while everyone is starting the next set, you don't play that set. And if that happens twice, people notice." Being seen struggling with tech *in front of peers* undermines musician credibility in trad culture.
- **Previous failed attempts:** Cleartune (works at home, useless in Whelan's on a Friday — community-listener.md #9 Peterson similar complaint), clip-on tuners (vibration crosstalk from table; "I looked ridiculous"), PitchLab ("cockpit instrument panel"), guitar tuner apps ("ignoring the strings I don't need — a humiliating workaround for a skilled musician").

### Behaviors
- **Current workaround:** Tuning forks. Tunes A or D from the fork, derives the rest by fifths (community-listener.md #25–26 — same 5th-fret-method banjo players use).
- **Tools:** iPhone, Tunefox, TheSession.org Safari bookmark, OAIM (Online Academy of Irish Music), WhatsApp for session scheduling, Voice Memos for tune-by-ear learning, Pro Metronome, Spotify.
- **Online communities:** TheSession.org (primary hub — thousands of lurkers per thread, [social-analyst.md]), Irish music Facebook groups, Comhaltas forums, OAIM, IrishBanjoLessons Discord (~400 GDAE players).
- **Decision triggers:** A respected session player handing her a phone. A post on TheSession. An Enda Scahill or Lisa Canny endorsement = instant install.

### A Day in Their Life
Eileen finishes school at 3pm, grabs her banjo case and octave mandolin bag, and heads to Hughes' pub for the Tuesday session. She arrives 15 minutes early but the pub is already loud — football match, conversation, radiator hiss. She opens her case, digs for the A tuning fork — it has slipped through the case lining again. She strikes it on her knee. Gets A sorted, derives D, G, E by fifths. The pub is too loud; she can't tell if the G is right. Between first and second tune in the set, she adjusts — but by then she has already sat out the first tune "like a lemon." Self-conscious about the G for the rest of the night.

### In Their Own Words
> "The frustration isn't really about the tuning itself. It's about the 15 seconds of fumbling and searching before you can even start tuning. In a session, those 15 seconds are everything."
*Source: v2 user-personas.md, Siobhan K. interview Q2*

> *"Some players prefer GDAE tuning, noting that having the low G is very important and provides immediate access to everything available in mandolin notation/tab."*
*Source: community-listener.md, quote #43 (Mandolin Cafe)*

> *"Your playing style will be more important than the tuning of your instrument."*
*Source: community-listener.md, quote #42 (The Session forum elder)*

> "There's an unspoken thing where if you're seen struggling with technology, it undermines your credibility as a musician. The tool needs to be invisible."
*Source: v2 user-personas.md, Siobhan K. interview Q10*

### Product Fit
- **Why she'd choose it:** Reference tones work in noisy pubs where mic tuners cannot (community-listener.md #9–10; competitor-analyst.md hardware §). If GDAE were a first-class preset — not buried in settings — she'd switch from the fork instantly. Phone doesn't roll under tables.
- **Why she'd walk away:** **No GDAE = no install.** If the home screen shows DGBD with no obvious tuning picker, she's gone in five seconds. Full-screen ad on launch = instant delete. Account creation = delete. Anything that adds latency to the fumble gap.
- **Willingness to pay:** EUR 4–5 without hesitation. "I spend more than that on a pint." Compares to EUR 24/year on lost forks.

**Hidden insight (carried from v2, re-validated):** Her tuning fork is *not* a bad tool — it is acoustically perfect. Banjen's competitive advantage against the fork is not sound quality; it is **losability**. A phone in a pocket does not roll under a pub table. Position against losability, not against accuracy.

## Persona 4: Wendell P., 71 — *The CGBD Plectrum Veteran (Evolved from Betty)*

*Retired Dixieland-band plectrum-banjo player in New Orleans whose 40-year ear is faster than his arthritic fingers and his Android phone combined.*

**Location:** New Orleans, Louisiana
**Income:** $42,000/year (pension)
**Tech savviness:** **1/5** — calls and texts only. Phone set up by his son Darnell. Diagnosed technology anxiety: fear of accidental purchases, learned avoidance.
**Decision authority:** Full on small purchases. Darnell is the de-facto gatekeeper for app installation.

**Research evidence trail:**
- [market-analyst.md] §Segment Sizing — 4-string plectrum CGBD ~3–8k global; tiny but vocal; "close (3 of 4)" overlap with DGBD assets.
- [community-listener.md] Theme 1 (#1–7) — friction-peg overshoot, compensated bridges, "close enough is usually good enough"; Theme 4 (#28, #32) — older-adult vision/fine-motor friction.
- [competitor-analyst.md] §Direct Competitor Table — no plectrum-specific tuners; all 11 reviewed apps default to 5-string. **CGBD is a real gap.**
- [academic-researcher.md] §4 — touch targets 60dp with 16dp spacing reduce error rates ~70% in 60+ cohorts (Jin et al., 2007); concrete labelled icons (Petrovčič et al., 2018); error-tolerant interaction; §6 — presbycusis 30% prevalence at 60–69, 53% at 70+; high-frequency loss affects perceived brightness of upper D4 string.
- [social-analyst.md] §Demographic Signals — English-speaking banjo searchers skew older (50+), male-heavy; clawhammer/old-time post-pandemic adult-beginner surge.

### Goals
- **Primary:** Keep his plectrum banjo in CGBD for weekly Dixieland-band rehearsals — the band is his primary social lifeline since his wife Marie passed.
- **Secondary:** Stop arriving 20 minutes early to ask Marcus the pianist for reference notes; reclaim his independence as a musician.
- **Hidden:** The band is his healthcare. His GP suggested "stay active with music" after a mild depression screening; some weeks Tuesday rehearsal is the only reason he gets dressed and leaves the house. **Tuning friction threatens a clinical outcome** — corroborated by academic-researcher.md's instrument-cognition evidence base.

### Pain Points
- **Biggest frustration:** 40 years of relative pitch — he just needs ONE reliable reference note and he can tune the whole banjo in 30 seconds. But every existing tool gives him needles he can't read or ads he's terrified of clicking.
- **Daily friction:** Arthritis limits practice to 15–30 minutes before pain. Every minute spent wrestling a tuner is stolen from a shrinking arthritis window. His fingers don't grip pegs as well — friction-peg overshoot (community-listener.md #2) is now a physical handicap, not a learning curve.
- **What keeps him up at night:** That declining eyesight, stiffening fingers, and tech-unfamiliarity will force him to give up playing — and with it, his weekly social connection. "If I had to drop out... I don't want to think about that."
- **Previous failed attempts:** Pitch pipe (worked; lost it in a move — Marie bought it). Snark clip-on ("the letters changed so fast I couldn't read them. I felt stupid, and I'm not stupid"). A free tuner app (accidentally clicked an ad, downloaded something, panicked, called Darnell to "clean it up," never opened that app again).

### Behaviors
- **Current workaround:** Arrives 20 minutes early to rehearsal; asks Marcus the pianist to play C-G-B-D so he can tune by ear. He calls this "the early-bird tuning club." Robert the trombonist does the same.
- **Tools:** Old Samsung Galaxy, printed songbook, physical metronome. **Not online.**
- **Online communities:** **None.** He is not online. Acquisition must happen through Darnell, bandmates, or his GP / occupational therapist via the emerging "social prescribing" channel.
- **Decision triggers:** Darnell physically installs the app and demonstrates it. A bandmate hands him a phone. His occupational therapist says "there's an app that plays your notes." One-sentence explainability: "Press the button, hear the note, tune to match."

### A Day in Their Life
Wendell wakes at 7am, takes his arthritis medication, waits for it to kick in. Around 10am he picks up his plectrum — 20 minutes is all his hands give him. He strums a chord and winces — the 2nd string sounds off. He picks up the phone, presses the side button, swipes up, squints at the icons. Taps what he thinks is a tuner. Banner ads everywhere. His instinct: don't touch anything ("last time I touched the wrong thing I ended up downloading something"). He freezes, puts the phone down, picks up the banjo anyway. Plays for 12 minutes feeling not the music but the tuning. Resolves, again, to get to rehearsal early Thursday and ask Marcus.

### In Their Own Words
> "I was charge nurse on a cardiac unit. I just don't have a mental map for how these things work, and every bad experience makes me less willing to try again."
*Source: v2 user-personas.md, Betty L. interview Q7 (evolved into Wendell)*

> *"The frustration of friction pegs is that they have a 1:1 ratio, so when you're close to the correct pitch, you have a tendency to jump past the correct position."*
*Source: community-listener.md, quote #2 (banjomountain.io)*

> *"Close enough is usually good enough."*
*Source: community-listener.md, quote #7 (Old Hickory, Banjo Hangout) — the wisdom of veteran ears.*

> *"Many extremely intrusive ads, full-screen popups which cannot be closed."*
*Source: community-listener.md, quote #17 (Perfect Tuner Google Play review) — the kind of ad that triggers his accidental-tap fear.*

### Product Fit
- **Why he'd choose it:** Big buttons that play the actual sound. Matches how he's tuned for 40 years. Looping playback lets him tune two-handed ("I can't hold the phone and turn a peg at the same time"). Warm tone, not a robot beep. Crucially: **no ads near tappable controls** (a single accidental click reinforces the avoidance spiral).
- **Why he'd walk away:** If the tuning is DGBD with no CGBD option — literally the wrong notes. If ads sit anywhere near the string buttons — accidental click = permanent deletion + reinforced avoidance. Touch targets too small for arthritic fingers. Hamburger menus, gear icons, settings screens. Any permission he doesn't understand.
- **Willingness to pay:** $5–10 if Darnell completes the purchase. One-time only — subscriptions are incomprehensible.

**Hidden insight (carried from v2, re-validated by academic-researcher.md §6):** He hears the banjo *better with his hearing aid removed* — the aid distorts high frequencies (4–8kHz) that the aid's own algorithms struggle with. Phone-speaker *clarity at close range* matters more than raw volume. Counter-intuitive design requirement: optimise for the speaker held 12 inches from the head, not for room-fill.

## Persona 5: Marcus T., 28 — *The Skeptic / Acquired-and-Lost (RETAINED, evolved)*

*Multi-instrumentalist studio musician in Austin who downloaded Banjen, hit an ad-layout shift during active tuning, deleted it, and now uses GStrings (free) and a $12 Snark clip-on. He embodies every download that doesn't convert.*

**Location:** Austin, Texas
**Income:** $38,000/year (part-time barista + gig musician)
**Tech savviness:** **5/5** — DAWs, audio interfaces, opinions about app architecture. Will write a 1-star review when annoyed.
**Decision authority:** Full, extremely price-sensitive. Uses a free 80%-good tool over a paid 100%-good tool, every time.

**Research evidence trail:**
- [community-listener.md] Pattern A ("Don't break the tuning surface") — universal across GuitarTuna, Cifra Club, Tuner ONE, Perfect Tuner, Pano Tuner. Marcus is the embodied case.
- [competitor-analyst.md] §Top-3 Teardown — **LikeTones (4.7★, "No Ads, No Purchases, No Registration")** is the philosophical competitor that already meets Marcus's bar. He has somewhere else to go.
- [competitor-analyst.md] §Hardware — a $12 Snark and a free GStrings set the minimum bar. Banjen's only structural moat against hardware is the reference-tone mode (clip-ons cannot make sound).
- [academic-researcher.md] §3 — Pham et al. (2024) scoping review of app abandonment: 70% within 100 days; top drivers are "poor UX (chiefly ads/interruptions)" and "evolving needs/goals."
- [social-analyst.md] §Demographic Signals — younger US session/studio musicians are Reddit-native, gear-review-driven (r/WeAreTheMusicMakers, r/banjo); not Banjo Hangout demographic.

### Goals
- **Primary:** Tune any instrument in under 30 seconds — tuning is overhead that kills creative momentum.
- **Secondary:** Switch between instruments mid-session (guitar / banjo / cavaquinho / mandolin) without re-configuring his tuner each time.
- **Hidden:** Leverage his guitar ear across instruments rather than learning instrument-specific skills. He plays cavaquinho with an Austin Brazilian music group — making the DGBD/cavaquinho overlap genuinely useful to him personally and a *second* validation of Lúcia's market thesis.

### Pain Points
- **Biggest frustration:** Ad layout shift during active tuning. "I'm holding my banjo up to my phone, adjusting the peg, and a banner appears, layout shifts, my finger hits the ad, Chrome opens." Specific, reproducible UX failure — and the *only* one. The rest of the app was perfect to him.
- **Daily friction:** Multi-instrument workflow constantly context-switches between tuner configurations. GStrings = 3 menus deep, manual D-G-B-D each time, 2 minutes per instrument. By the time he's tuned, "the creative spark has faded."
- **What keeps him up at night:** Whether banjo tracks actually sound in tune in his recordings. He records with A=432Hz artists and "literally has to use a DAW to generate reference tones at 432" — no Banjen-class app currently has adjustable reference pitch as a default.
- **Previous failed attempts:** Banjen (deleted after one session — ad layout shift). GuitarTuna (subscription is offensive; banjo behind paywall — corroborated by competitor-analyst.md). GStrings (functional but slow). LikeTones ("fine, not great" — but no ads).

### Behaviors
- **Current workaround:** Boss TU-3 pedal tuner ($100) for electric. GStrings for acoustic — slow, manual. His friend Diego skipped software entirely with a $12 Snark on the headstock.
- **Tools:** Pixel 8, Ableton Live, Focusrite Scarlett 2i2, KRK monitors, Boss TU-3, GStrings, occasionally LikeTones.
- **Online communities:** Reddit r/WeAreTheMusicMakers, r/banjo (occasionally), YouTube gear reviews, Bandcamp, Instagram for band promo. **Not** on Banjo Hangout, **not** on TheSession.
- **Decision triggers:** Reddit thread recommendation. YouTube gear review. A friend at a gig. Free is non-negotiable. He has three explicit reinstall conditions: (1) no ads during tuning flow, (2) homescreen widget, (3) adjustable reference pitch A=432–446Hz. Miss any one and he stays on GStrings.

### A Day in Their Life
Marcus finishes his morning shift at the coffee shop and heads to his buddy Rafa's home studio at 3pm. They're tracking a Brazilian choro arrangement — cavaquinho and banjo, both DGBD. His Boss TU-3 is wired for electric guitar. He pulls out his phone, opens GStrings, scrolls to chromatic, plucks the D — Rafa's AC hums, monitor bleed, fridge from the next room — needle bounces. Two minutes per instrument, four total. Rafa's already tracked his guitar and is looking at him: "bro, are we doing this?" The creative spark for the banjo part fades. Mediocre take.

### Product Fit
- **Why he'd choose it (again):** Core idea is perfect — instant reference tones, zero configuration, faster than hardware *if* the widget ships. Cavaquinho overlap is a genuine bonus. Reference tones work where mic tuners fail.
- **Why he stays gone:** LikeTones already exists with "No Ads, No Purchases, No Registration" (competitor-analyst.md). Banjen has a narrow window to ship the ad-free tuning surface before LikeTones takes the rest of his cohort. His three-condition list is non-negotiable. If Banjen reinstalls and ads shift the layout once, "it's gone forever."
- **Willingness to pay:** $3–5 one-time for something that genuinely replaces his workflow. Would tolerate a 5-second cold-start ad if the tuning screen was permanently clean. The comparison point isn't other apps — it's a $12 piece of plastic.

**Hidden insight (carried from v2):** He has *already designed the correct monetization strategy*: ads everywhere except the tuning screen. He is not anti-monetization. He is anti-interruption. The revenue model and the UX model are not in conflict if ads are spatially separated from the active tuning flow. (competitor-analyst.md confirms: Ultimate Banjo Tuner's "one-time IAP ad-free" model is the most-respected upsell in the category.)

## Proposed Primary Persona for v3 — **Harold M.**

**Harold M. remains the beachhead.** Three reasons, all freshly grounded in v3 research:

1. **Banjen already serves him with zero code changes.** The DGBD reference tones literally are his ideal product. No new tunings, no new screens, no new features. (market-analyst.md confirms the addressable pool; competitor-analyst.md confirms the "reference-tone-first, no mic permission, no signup" position is uniquely defensible.)
2. **Academic literature now corroborates his hidden motivation.** Balbag (2014), Bisiacchi (2025), and Bugos (2022) make the cognitive-maintenance value proposition defensible — not a marketing pose. (academic-researcher.md §2.) This raises the *stake* of every tuning friction from "annoyance" to "interruption of a health-relevant habit."
3. **His tolerance for the current product's limitations is the highest of any persona.** He doesn't need GDAE, CGBD, widgets, A=432, or session mode. He needs four big buttons, looping tones, and no ads during play. The gap between "what Harold needs" and "what Banjen ships today" is the smallest gap on this list.

Lúcia G. is the strongest **secondary** persona — she is the highest-leverage *growth* persona (zero-code pt-BR ASO unlocks her), but she is not the bar for product correctness. The bar for product correctness is whether Harold continues his 7am routine.
