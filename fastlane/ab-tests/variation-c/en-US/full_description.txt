Banjen is a simple tuner for banjo and cavaquinho — both instruments share the same DGBD tuning. Tap a button, hear the note, tune your string to match. Whether you play samba, pagode, choro, or old-time banjo, you're covered.

One app. Two instruments. Four strings. Same perfect tuning.

HOW IT WORKS

Open the app and you'll see four big buttons — one for each string. Tap a button and it plays a clear, looping tone. Pluck your string and turn the tuning peg until the sounds match. That's it.

No confusing displays, no microphone needed, no sign-up. Just tap, listen, and tune.

YOUR TUNING

Banjen plays standard DGBD tuning — the shared tuning for 4-string banjo and cavaquinho:

• String 4 (thickest) — D (Ré)
• String 3 — G (Sol)
• String 2 — B (Si)
• String 1 (thinnest) — D (Ré)

This is the same tuning used by pagode legends like Almir Guineto and Fundo de Quintal, and by 4-string banjo players everywhere.

THE BANJO-CAVAQUINHO CONNECTION

The Brazilian banjo was born in the 1970s, when samba musicians like Mussum and Almir Guineto fused the American banjo with the cavaquinho. The result was the banjo de samba — an instrument with the banjo's body and the cavaquinho's DGBD tuning. That's why one app tunes both instruments perfectly.

TUNE BY EAR — THE WAY THE MASTERS DO

In samba and pagode, tuning by ear is a tradition. The great musicians didn't rely on digital displays — they listened. Banjen follows that same tradition: instead of using your phone's microphone, it plays the actual tone for each string so you can hear and match it.

Every time you tune by ear, your musical ear gets stronger.
